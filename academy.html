Building practical, trauma-informed homicide response capacity

Stand The Gap Academy is being developed to provide education, professional development, and practical tools for people and organizations supporting families after homicide and violent loss.

Future learning pathways

Social workers and advocates

  • Responding to homicide loss
  • Crisis stabilization
  • Family and benefits navigation
  • Court advocacy fundamentals

Hospitals and health systems

  • Emergency-department family support
  • Post-homicide discharge planning
  • Resource coordination
  • Trauma-informed communication

Law enforcement and counties

  • Supporting families beyond notification
  • Referral pathways
  • Long-term family needs
  • Cross-system collaboration

Schools and youth programs

  • Childhood traumatic grief
  • Supporting students after violence
  • Family collaboration
  • Community resource connection

Funeral professionals

  • Trauma-informed family meetings
  • Victim-benefit awareness
  • Communication after violent death
  • Resource referral practices

Community organizations

  • Homicide response planning
  • Survivor advocacy
  • Compassion fatigue prevention
  • Community healing coordination
