How We Made an Impact

Families received individualized advocacy, navigation, and support tailored to their needs following homicide.

Insurance Benefits Navigation

Successfully assisted families in obtaining employer-sponsored life insurance and accidental-death benefits.

Crime Victims Reparations

Assisted families in establishing ongoing reparations benefits, including successful back-pay awards and continued monthly compensation when eligible.

Funeral & Cremation Assistance

Helped families locate funding and navigate available resources related to funeral and cremation costs.

Court Advocacy

Provided court accompaniment, emotional support, and criminal-justice navigation during homicide proceedings.

Long-Term Family Support

Continued supporting families beyond the immediate crisis through legal, financial, and family milestones.

Resource & System Navigation

Helped families understand options, organize documentation, connect with resources, and follow through on complex processes.

Barriers Families May Face

Homicide loss can create emotional, financial, legal, and practical challenges at the same time. Stand The Gap helps families identify available options and move through complicated systems.

Lack of Benefit Awareness

Families may not know that victim reparations, employer benefits, accidental-death coverage, survivor benefits, or funeral resources may be available.

Complex Applications

Families may be expected to complete forms, gather records, meet deadlines, and communicate with multiple systems while actively grieving.

Criminal-Justice Stress

Court hearings and homicide trials can be difficult to understand and emotionally overwhelming without consistent support.
